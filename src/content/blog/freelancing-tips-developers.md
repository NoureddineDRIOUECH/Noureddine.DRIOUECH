After transitioning from full-time employment to freelancing, I've learned a lot about what works and what doesn't. Here's my advice for developers considering the freelance path.

## Finding Your First Clients

The hardest part is getting started. Here's what worked for me:

1. **Update your portfolio** — Showcase your best work with case studies
2. **Use platforms wisely** — Upwork and LinkedIn are great starting points
3. **Network authentically** — Attend meetups and engage in developer communities
4. **Start small** — Take on smaller projects to build testimonials

## Pricing Your Work

Don't undersell yourself. Calculate your rates based on:
- Your experience level
- Project complexity
- Market rates in your niche
- The value you deliver to the client

## Managing Projects

Clear communication is everything. I use:
- Notion for documentation and specs
- GitHub for version control
- Weekly updates to keep clients informed

## Conclusion

Freelancing offers incredible freedom, but it requires discipline. Start with a solid foundation, deliver quality work, and build lasting relationships with your clients.
